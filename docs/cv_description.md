## Strong CV version

**RogueLens: Microlensing Simulator for Rogue Planets**  
Developed a Python-based astrophysics simulation that models point-lens gravitational microlensing events caused by free-floating planets, generating light curves, estimating Einstein crossing times, and comparing Earth-, Neptune-, and Jupiter-mass scenarios.

## Activities list version

Created RogueLens, an independent Python project exploring rogue planets and gravitational microlensing. Implemented the point-lens magnification model, generated simulated light curves, added synthetic observation noise, and wrote documentation explaining the physics and limitations of the model.

## Interview explanation

I wanted to understand how astronomers can detect objects that are almost invisible. Rogue planets are difficult to observe directly because they are not orbiting a bright star. I learned that gravitational microlensing can reveal them through a temporary increase in the brightness of a background star. I implemented the basic equations in Python, produced light curves, compared different lens masses, and documented what the model can and cannot explain.
