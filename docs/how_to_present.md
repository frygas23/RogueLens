# How to present RogueLens in a CV or interview

## One-sentence explanation

RogueLens is a Python simulator that models how a free-floating planet can briefly magnify the light of a background star through gravitational microlensing.

## Simple spoken explanation

Some planets may drift through space without orbiting a star. Because they do not shine, they are hard to detect directly. But if one passes almost exactly in front of a distant star, its gravity bends and magnifies the star's light. RogueLens simulates that temporary brightening as a light curve.

## What to emphasize

- I connected physics, mathematics, and programming.
- I implemented the point-lens microlensing equations in Python.
- I compared how mass and closest approach change the light curve.
- I added plots, documentation, tests, and export tools.
- I clearly stated the limitations of the model.

## What not to overclaim

Do not say that RogueLens discovers real rogue planets. It is an educational simulator, not a professional survey pipeline.

## Strong CV bullet

Built **RogueLens**, a Python-based astrophysics simulator modeling point-lens gravitational microlensing events caused by free-floating planets; generated light curves, estimated Einstein crossing times, compared planetary-mass scenarios, and documented the model's assumptions and limitations.
